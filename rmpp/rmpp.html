Research Methods and Professional Practice

 

To provide a practical foundation in research methods that will help the students to conduct research in a practical setting.

1.    It covers various tools and techniques that a researcher may need and allows for feedback throughout the module through cases and project tasks

2.    It provides essential insight into the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues applicable to computing projects and the relevant codes of practice and practices.

 

Learning Outcomes:

-Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.

-Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the relevant computing field.

-Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.

-Produce and evaluate a research proposal for the chosen topic critically.

 

 

Unit 1: Scientific Investigation and Ethics

 

In this unit, I will learn what the Purpose of Research is.

The three primary purposes of research are:

1.1: to explore (familiarise yourself with a topic/situation)

1.2: to describe (describe your findings),

1.3: to explain (answer the why)

 

One of the essential approaches in research is the scientific method which involves the following:

-Observation,

-Hypothesis,

-Reasoning and

-Testing

 

This unit focuses on the types of reasoning used in the scientific method, as these are the fundamental approaches for problem-solving.

Unit 12: Project Management and Managing Risk

 

In this unit, I will be considering project management and how to manage risk within a project through adequate reporting and control tools.

I will also be introduced to some principal methodologies for managing projects.

 

What is Project Management?

Project management is the controlling of processes and activities that are related to a project

 

Managing Risk

A risk-free project does not exist, and it is impossible to avoid the risks entirely. Many risks are not visible at the start of a project, but the most significant potential risks need to be calculated and planned.

 

The unit will also cover managing the inevitable changes within a project's life. What can frequently happen during project implementation is that a need or desire for change is identified. To ensure that the impact of change is minimised and/or to determine how it will impact the project plan, scope, and outcomes, the Project Manager must control changes through a change control process.

 

Risk and change management is vital to ensure that project quality is maintained and to minimise impact on project cost and planned time frames whilst taking advantage of opportunities which may be identified during the process.
