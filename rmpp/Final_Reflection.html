Unit 1 Reflection on Scientific Investigation and Ethics

This unit focuses on Scientific Investigation and Ethics. As for the first unit, I thought it would be easy, but I was wrong as I struggled to comprehend the difference between Deductive and Inductive reasoning; I did poorly on the reasoning quiz.

 

I have learned that knowing codes of ethics and professional conduct and living them out day-to-day are very different (Kaptein, 2017).

 

Unit 2 Reflection on Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

Besides working on the Unit 1 discussion forum, I have started with the Unit 2 reading. I was thinking about utilising and adopting IoT technologies in secondary education in Singapore, as I could use online surveys to gather data from teachers, parents and students.

 

After reading the material, I realised that research is a tedious process that is essential to ensure successful research. It is not about providing the correct answers but struggling to find the right questions (Phillips & Pugh, 2010).

 

Unit 3 Reflection on Research Methods

In this unit, I was introduced to different research methods. At the same time, it was made known during seminar three that primary research cannot be used in the literature review; it confused me, and I emailed the tutor to seek clarification.

 

I have a better understanding of qualitative and quantitative data collection, as well as the literature review topic must have solid existing data/research available. Given this constraint, I have to reconsider my literature review topic.

 

Also, by revisiting Unit 1, I have a further understanding that my literature can be inductive, deductive or both (Bandara et al., 2015)

 

Unit 4 Reflection on Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

In this unit, I was introduced to some data collection methods used for qualitative and quantitative research and, at the same time to prepare a brief outline of the literature review.

Due to my busy work schedule, I forgot about the deadline and did not submit it on time; I tried to email the outline to the tutor but was rejected.

 

I was disappointed, but at the same time, I have learned to respect the submission deadline and monitor future assignment deadlines closely.

 

Unit 5 Reflection on Interviews and Survey Methods

In this unit, I have to do a case study on the inappropriate use of surveys. After completing the case study, I have learned that a well-designed survey must be ethical and take data security, privacy, transparency and legal into consideration. Also, all involved parties must act professionally, i.e. to respect the BCS code of conduct (BCS, 2022) and ACM code of ethics (ACM, 2022)

 

Unit 6 Reflection on Questionnaire Design

In this unit, I emailed two supervisors so that they could provide feedback on the viability of my proposed topic, but I did not get any response from them, and I was disappointed. I have reached out to my tutor for help and was offered some advice on this matter.

Unit 7 Reflection on Validity and Generalisability in Research

In this unit, I have learned the various techniques used for data analysis in qualitative and quantitative research, which is a complex topic for me.

 

At the same time, I have begun with Collaborative Learning Discussion 2 and have three peer responses. As a result, I have learned to accept different perspectives and respond professionally to my peers when making a counter-peer response.

 

 

Unit 8 Reflection on Inferential Statistics

This topic is a challenging unit as it deals with statistics. I knew I had learned statistics before, but it was at least twenty years ago.

 

I have to allocate extra time to read the requirements carefully and put in additional effort to understand what is required while going through the example worksheets. As a result, I was able to build up my confidence level before attempting the exercise worksheets. Eventually, I completed the exercises but needed more time to interpret the results.

 

Through the process, I have learned the concept of inference and hypothesis testing and how to keep going.

 

Unit 9 Reflection on Analysing Qualitative Data

In this unit, I was introduced to various methods available for analysing qualitative data and from the previous experiences gained from Unit 8, I felt more confident in handling this topic. As a result, it took me less time to complete the sample examples and the exercises, and the interpretation of the findings was much easier than before.

 

The feedback I received from the tutor was that I had completed it to a very good standard, which was a morale booster for me.

 

Using the findings, I have learned to present a clear message to my target audience.

 

 

Unit 10 Reflection on Research Writing

During Seminar 5, I was pleased to know that I could use the same topic in the literature review for the research proposal as I struggled with work commitment.

However, I have to ensure that the content is different from the Literature review to avoid any risk of self-plagiarising.

To ensure that I was on the right track, I had to make sure that the research proposal outline was submitted before the deadline.

 

In this unit, I have learned to rely only on secondary research literature, even though I was very tempted to use primary research. I have also learned the importance of taking ethical considerations in designing the questionnaire and engaging the participants should primary research should be conducted. Finally, another valuable lesson for me is to ensure that the proposal is focused on deep research and develop criticality in the content.
